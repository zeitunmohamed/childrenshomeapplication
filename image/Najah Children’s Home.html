BACK GROUND INFORMATION

Najah Children’s Home is an orphanage with humble inception that was registered by the Registrar General of the Societies SOC/1984, Registration No 11520 of 18th January 1986. The founding fathers were moved by the increasing number of needy Muslim orphans that were languishing in poverty with no care and no hope as the area had only one orphanage and could not cater for all the orphans. They pulled their resources together and rent some few rooms in a residential premises in Garissa town and admitted the first batch of 60 orphans.

The founders were well- visioned and acquired land in 1989 for the institution in the Iftin Location of Garissa County for they were quite optimistic that someday the institution would grow.

By the grace of Allah the orphanage got good will support for the construction of six rooms and a dining hall from the organization of Islamic Heritage & Culture.

Later in 1993 (1412 A.H) a dormitory with a capacity of 145 orphans was built and the same number of orphans sponsored. A mosque was also built to cater for 250 persons.

In1994 (1413 A.H) the Qatari Charity generously put up three dormitories to settle 150 orphans, an administration block and to sponsored 150 school-based orphans and others through the home based care and support project. Currently the centre supports 230 Orphans from diverse background representing all communities in Kenya.

To empower children by providing a safe environment, access to education, and the tools they need to thrive and succeed.
To create a world where every child has the opportunity to lead a happy, healthy, and fulfilling life.

Economic Viability

The construction of a Seven-Side Football Field at Najah Children’s Home will serve as a sustainable investment for the institution and the community. It will:

  • Provide a recreational facility for the children, promoting their physical and mental health.
  • Attract partnerships and sponsorships from sports and wellness organizations.
  • Generate income through rental opportunities for community sports events and local leagues.
  • Encourage community engagement and cohesion, creating a sense of belonging.
  • Foster talent development, paving the way for future sports professionals.
